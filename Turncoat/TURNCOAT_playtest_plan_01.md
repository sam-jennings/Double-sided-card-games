# TURNCOAT — Playtest Plan 01

*Pairs with the forthcoming `TURNCOAT_playtest_01.md`. Written per `.kiro/steering/playtesting.md`.*

## Setup

- **Game:** TURNCOAT
- **Version:** v1.2 (balanced over ~22k simulated games; never tabled).
- **Players:** 2 (testable count per `focus-priority.md` rung 2 — 18 turns each).
- **Symbol subset:** all 9 signs / 36 cards. **Use the sim-balanced preset teams** rather than drafting on a first game: 🔥⭐🌱🌊 vs 🎭👁👑⚫, with 🗝 Key **unaligned**.
- **Why this session:** TURNCOAT shares TRIGON's engine but is "opposite soul" — directed, personal conflict. It is sim-tuned but has had **zero plays**. First human contact; the question is whether the allegiance/defection layer feels good and teaches, not whether the numbers hold.

## Hypotheses to test

Recorded first-table questions (design analysis §8, reproduced in `COLLECTION_AUDIT.md`), framed as falsifiable predictions:

- **H1 — One activation per turn stays exciting over 18 turns.** *Prediction:* turn-to-turn engagement holds to the end; no stretch in the back half where turns feel like rote placement with no meaningful flip/extract decision.
- **H2 — The bury-your-own (covering) loop teaches itself.** Covering your *own* exposed agent banks it to you; covering a *rival's* hands them the point. *Prediction:* players grasp within the first few turns that they mostly bury their own, without needing a hard warning — OR this needs the "covering rivals gifts them points" rule stated more forcefully (record which).
- **H3 — Mask is a fun strong pick, not a must-pick.** Mask flips any agent in its row/column when adjacent to a rival. *Prediction:* with Mask pre-assigned to one preset team, the Mask team does not feel auto-dominant; the other team has answers.
- **H4 — Allegiance is legible at a glance.** Loyalty = the showing face; allegiances are public. *Prediction:* both players track "whose agent is that?" by reading faces against their claimed signs, with no hidden-state confusion. *(`physical-handling.md`: visible-face allegiance, claim verifiable on demand.)*

## What to watch

- **Banking vs squatting tension.** "An exposed agent is a promise you haven't kept" — 2 endgame points if it survives, 1 banked point if you cash it. Watch whether players feel this trade-off or default to one mode.
- **Mercenary abilities cutting both ways** — does a player place a Flame/Mask for themselves only to have the opponent swing it next turn? Is that "aha" or "feel-bad"?
- **Extract vs cover distinction** (Key/Crown empty a post; covering replaces and banks) — a likely comprehension snag. Watch for misplays.
- **The unaligned Key.** Unaligned faces "make sturdy walls" and covering one removes it. Watch whether the neutral sign creates interesting walls or just confusion.
- **Chain resolution** — same handling risk as TRIGON: losing track of charged agents mid-chain.
- **Final-grid scoring** (2 pts per surviving agent of your signs) — does the endgame board-hold incentive read clearly?

## Observable success / failure signals

| | Holds (success) | Fails |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Decisions stay live to turn 18; no rote stretch | Mid/late turns feel like filler placement |
| H2 | Players self-correct toward burying own agents early | Repeatedly gift rivals points; rule needs hardening |
| H3 | Mask team not runaway; flips contested both ways | Mask feels mandatory / decides the game alone |
| H4 | Allegiance read off faces unaided | Confusion over whose agent is whose; needs recounting |
| Handling | Cover/extract distinction applied correctly | Repeated misplays; chains lose their state |

## Teaching plan

- Teach **allegiance first**: your signs are public, an agent's loyalty is its showing face, flipping defects it. This is the game's hook and distinguishes it from TRIGON.
- Use **preset teams** (skip the snake-draft) — the rulebook recommends this for a first game and the teams are sim-balanced.
- Consider the **Loyalists variant** (you may only activate agents of your *own* signs) for the opening game — "fewer fireworks, cleaner reads" — then open up to mercenary activation once the engine is understood. Decide at the table based on how the first few turns land.
- **Watch during teaching:** whether "covering a rival banks the point to *them*" needs saying twice (direct H2 signal).

## Out of scope

- **Allied Houses (2v2) at 4P** — a recorded question, but **not testable at 2P**; defer to a later session with 4 players. Note it explicitly so it isn't lost.
- Snake-draft balance — using presets this session; draft is a separate later question.
- Bounty variant unless time permits after a clean game.
- Re-simulation — numbers are covered; the table is the priority (`playtesting.md`).
