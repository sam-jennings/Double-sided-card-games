# TURNCOAT

*A game of shifting allegiance for 2–4 players · ~15–25 minutes · ages 10+*

Nine signs mark the agents of the field — and every agent bears two. Each card in the deck carries two signs, one per face: whichever face shows is where its loyalty lies *today*. Turn an agent and they defect. Bury one and they bank to their sign's owner. Bank the most agents for your signs and you win.

---

## Components

- **36 double-faced cards.** There are 9 signs. Every possible *pair* of two different signs appears on exactly one card (one sign per face). Each sign therefore marks exactly 8 cards.
- This rulebook (the sign reference table is the only thing you'll need mid-game).

A full card manifest is at the end of this document.

---

## Setup

1. **Claim your signs.** Starting with the player who most recently changed their mind about something and going clockwise, snake-draft signs (1-2-2-1 order in 2P) until each player has their set:
   - **2 players:** 4 signs each; the last unclaimed sign is **unaligned**.
   - **3 players:** 3 signs each; none unaligned.
   - **4 players:** 2 signs each; the last is **unaligned**.
   Note your signs openly — allegiances are public.
   *First game? Skip the draft and use the preset teams at the end of the sign table.*
2. Shuffle the deck thoroughly, **flipping random chunks as you shuffle** so each card's face-up side is randomised.
3. Stack the deck — the top card's showing face is **public information**. Its hidden side is not.
4. Leave space for a **3×3 grid** of posts in the middle of the table. It starts empty. The drafter goes first; play proceeds clockwise.

With 2/3/4 players everyone gets exactly 18/12/9 turns — the deck divides evenly.

---

## Your turn

1. **Draw** the top card of the deck. Look at both sides secretly.
2. **Place** it on any post (cell), choosing which face shows:
   - **Empty post:** the agent simply takes up position.
   - **Occupied post — a covering:** the agent already there is **extracted**: it goes face-up into the **score pile of the player who owns its showing sign**, and your new card takes the post. (If the extracted face is unaligned, the card goes to a face-down **removed pile**, out of the game.)
3. **Resolve** activations and chains (below), in any order you choose.

When the deck runs out, finish that turn — the game ends.

**Covering is how points are scored.** Mostly you'll bury *your own* exposed agents to bank them. Covering a rival's agent hands *them* the point — legal, occasionally worth it for the post, almost always a gift.

---

## Agents, allegiance, and flipping

- An agent's **allegiance** is the sign on its showing face: yours, a rival's, or unaligned.
- **Flipping a card changes its allegiance** — same agent, new master. Flips only happen through sign abilities.
- Abilities are **mercenary**: on your turn you may activate *any* charged agent whose requirement is met, whatever its sign. Words like "you", "your agents" and "rival" in requirements and abilities always refer to **the active player**.
- Banking is **loyal**: an extracted agent always scores for the owner of its showing sign — no matter who buried it.

---

## Activations

Every sign has a **requirement** (about the agent's surroundings) and an **ability**. A card becomes **charged** when:

- it is just **placed**, or
- it is **flipped** or **moved** (by any ability), or
- its requirement **changes from unmet to met** during the turn.

During your resolution step you may **activate any charged agent whose requirement is currently met** — or decline. You choose the order. Each card activates **at most once per turn**. Cards already sitting with their requirement met at the start of your turn are *not* charged — their moment has passed.

Activations are **always optional**. *Adjacent* always means orthogonally adjacent (sharing an edge). Extracted and removed cards lose any pending activation.

### The 9 signs

| Sign | Requirement | Ability | Mnemonic |
|---|---|---|---|
| ⚫ **Void** (1) | A rival has **2+ more** banked cards than you | Choose such a rival: their **top banked card** is removed from the game | The Void erases — when you're behind, it devours what the leader hoarded |
| 🌱 **Root** (2) | Adjacent to one of **your** agents | **Swap** Root's card with any other agent; you may then **flip** the displaced agent at Root's old post | Deep roots among allies let you displace and overturn intruders |
| 🌊 **Wave** (3) | **Exactly one** adjacent agent | **Move** that agent to any empty post; you may then **flip** it | The Wave carries one thing away and tumbles it |
| 🔥 **Flame** (4) | Adjacent to a rival agent | **Remove** one adjacent rival agent from the game (its post empties) | Flame consumes a rival beside it |
| 👁 **Eye** (5) | At most **one** adjacent agent | **Peek** at the hidden face of an adjacent agent; you may then **flip** it | The Eye in solitude sees truth, and may reveal it |
| 🎭 **Mask** (6) | Adjacent to a rival agent | **Flip** any one agent in Mask's row or column | The Mask near an enemy turns a face in its line — identity is mutable |
| 🗝 **Key** (7) | Key's row **or** column holds **2+** of your agents | **Extract** one of your agents from Key's row or column straight to your pile (its post empties) | The Key among allies unlocks one of yours from the line |
| ⭐ **Star** (8) | **Two or more** adjacent agents | **Draw the next card** from the deck and play it immediately (full turn rules) | A Star well-connected draws the unexpected into play |
| 👑 **Crown** (9) | Adjacent to one of **your** agents | **Extract Crown's own card** to its sign-owner's pile (its post empties) | The Crown beside its allies withdraws to safety — sovereign departure |

*Flipped, moved, and swapped cards become charged and may chain. A worked chain:*

> Your Eye sits beside a lone rival 🎭 Mask. You activate Eye, peek, and flip the Mask — it turns out to be a 🗝 **Key** of yours, and Key's column already holds another of your agents: requirement newly met, the Key is charged. You activate Key and extract that other agent to your pile. The extraction emptied a post next to your 🌊 Wave, whose neighbour count just dropped to exactly one — charged. The Wave carries the last rival agent into the open and flips it. Three activations, one banked card, and a field that looks nothing like it did a minute ago.

## Game end & scoring

When the deck is empty, finish the current turn, then score:

- **1 point** per card in your score pile.
- **2 points** per agent of your signs still showing on the final grid — the field remembers who held the ground.
- Most points wins. **Tiebreaker 1:** whoever banked a card **most recently** (the freshest extraction). **Tiebreaker 2:** earliest in turn order.

---

## Edge cases & FAQ

**Can I cover a card mid-chain?** No. Covering happens only when you *place* your drawn card (including a card drawn by Star). Key and Crown extractions are not coverings — nothing replaces the agent, the post just empties.

**Star's extra card — full rules?** Yes: look at both sides, place on any post (covering banks as normal), resolve. A Star drawn by a Star can chain; the deck running dry ends the fun. If the deck empties mid-turn, finish the turn — the game then ends.

**Who can activate Crown?** Anyone, but the card always banks to *Crown's owner* — so in practice you'll only ever crown your own.

**Void with the scores level?** No — Void needs a rival at least 2 banked cards ahead of *you*, the active player.

**Wave with two neighbours?** Requirement unmet — Wave needs exactly one adjacent agent.

**Root's flip — which card?** The *other* one: after the swap, the displaced agent stands at Root's old post, and that's the card you may flip. Root itself keeps its face.

**Does covering charge anything?** The covered card is gone before anything can trigger. But the new card is charged by placement, and the change of face can flip neighbours' requirements from unmet to met — that charges them.

**A rival face I covered — who scores?** Its sign's owner. Always. Bury rivals only with your eyes open.

**Unaligned agents?** They're nobody's: covering one removes it from the game, and it never scores for the final grid. Unaligned faces make sturdy walls.

**Can the grid empty out?** It can shrink (Flame, Key, Crown, Void all thin the field) — placements refill it. You can always place somewhere.

## Strategy notes (read after your first game)

- **An exposed agent is a promise you haven't kept.** Your face on the board is two endgame points or one banked point — but only if it survives the table's flips. Bank early against flippers; squat late against bankers.
- **Flipping is tempo theft.** Every defection you force costs a rival the turn they spent showing that face.
- **The deck-top is public.** You always know one face of what your rival inherits. Plan your exposures around it.
- **Mercenaries cut both ways.** That Flame you placed for yourself is a Flame your rival may swing next turn. The board you leave matters more than the board you made.
- **Watch the Void gap.** Pull 2+ banked cards ahead and your pile becomes vulnerable. Sometimes the second-place seat is the safest place to sharpen knives.

## Designer's variants

- **Loyalists** (calmer, great first game): you may only activate agents of *your own* signs. Fewer fireworks, cleaner reads.
- **Bounty:** covering an unaligned agent banks it to *you* instead of removing it. Faster scoring; the neutral sign becomes a harvest everyone races for.
- **Allied Houses** (recommended for 4P): play 2 vs 2, partners sitting opposite, scores combined at the end. No table talk about hidden faces. Restores the two-sided tension of the duel at four players.
