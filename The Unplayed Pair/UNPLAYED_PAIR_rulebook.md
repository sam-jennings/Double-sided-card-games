# THE UNPLAYED PAIR

*A trick-taking whodunit for 3–5 players · ~25 minutes · ages 10+*

One card is missing. Dealt to no one, shown to no one — the **unplayed pair**. Every trick you win is a point, but the real prize is the missing card's name: call it early, while the table is still guessing, and the round is yours. Every trick ends with a reveal, every reveal kills possibilities, and the player who counts fastest doesn't need to win tricks at all.

---

## Components

- The **36-card** master deck. This game uses the corner **index pips (1–9)** printed on every face: each symbol's number is its rank, 9 highest.

## Setup

1. Shuffle thoroughly, flipping random chunks.
2. Slide one card off the deck **unseen by anyone** — touching only its edges — into the box or under the rulebook. That is the unplayed pair.
3. Deal the rest evenly: **3P: 11 each · 4P: 8 · 5P: 7**.
4. Place the leftovers face-up in the middle as the **morgue** (3P: 2 cards · 4P: 3 · 5P: none). Morgue cards are public and may be picked up and examined — *both sides* — by anyone at any time.
5. Examine your own hand (both sides) freely throughout. Player left of dealer leads the first trick.

## The trick

**Leading:** play any card, either face up. The visible symbol is the **led symbol**.

**Following:** in turn order, each player must play a card carrying the led symbol **on either face**, placed so the led symbol shows — flipping a hand card to its other face to follow is the game's signature move, and it's mandatory if that's the only way you can follow. If no card of yours carries the led symbol at all, **slough**: play any card, any face up. *(Sloughs can't win.)*

**The reveal:** when everyone has played, **flip every card in the trick** where it lies. This is the moment the game turns on:

- Among the cards that *followed* (showed the led symbol — including the leader's), the one whose revealed face has the **highest pip wins the trick**. Ties are impossible: every follower hid a different partner of the led symbol, because every pair exists exactly once.
- The reveal is also evidence. Every trick publishes three, four, five complete card identities. The morgue gave you more. Your hand gives you the rest. Somewhere in what's left is the unplayed pair.

The winner gathers the trick and places it **face-up and spread** in front of them. All won tricks stay public — anyone may examine any won card, either side, at any time. *Nothing in this game asks you to remember anything; it asks you to reason.*

The trick's winner leads the next.

## Calling the pair

Once per round, at the start of any trick — before its lead — any player (in turn order from the leader) may **call the unplayed pair**, naming both symbols aloud: *"The missing card is Crown-and-Key."* The call is settled at the end of the round when the unplayed card is revealed:

- **Right:** score **+1 per trick that had not yet been led** when you called (the current one counts). Calling before trick 5 of 8 with 4 tricks left pays 4.
- **Wrong:** score **−3**. And everyone heard you be wrong, which is its own tax — a wrong call is also *information*, and the table will use it.

One call per player per round. Calls are public and permanent.

## Scoring the round

Reveal the unplayed card. Score **+1 per trick won**, plus call results. Play a match of one round per player, rotating dealer; highest total wins. Tiebreak: most correct calls, then fewest wrong ones.

## Renege (it polices itself)

If the reveal shows the **led symbol on the hidden face of a slough**, that player could have followed and didn't: they take **−2 immediately**, and the trick's winner takes one card of their choice from the offender's won tricks (if any). Most reneges in this game are caught by the cards themselves the moment they flip.

## Edge cases & FAQ

**Everyone sloughed except the leader?** The leader wins the trick alone.

**Can I lead a card showing a symbol I'm void in otherwise?** Of course. Leading is always legal; the led symbol is whatever your visible face says.

**Must I win if I can?** Never. Following with your *low* partner to duck a trick is half the craft — but remember the reveal will show the table what you kept the lead away from.

**When following, I have several cards carrying the led symbol.** Play any one of them. Which partner you spend — and which you keep — is the other half of the craft.

**Can the led symbol appear on a revealed follower's face?** No. A follower *shows* the led symbol; its hidden face is that symbol's partner, which is never the symbol itself.

**Does a call change play?** Profoundly. A right-sounding call warns everyone to update; a wrong call sends them down your dead end. There is no rule against calling to deceive — a deliberate wrong call costs 3 and is occasionally worth every penny.

**5P seems tightest.** It is: no morgue, 7 tricks, and the deal puts every card but one in someone's hand. The deduction is purest there.

## Strategy notes (read after your first round)

- **Your hand is the biggest clue.** You start knowing 8–11 pairs that *aren't* missing (plus the morgue). The race is between your tricks-won engine and your elimination engine — they spend the same cards.
- **Every follow burns a partner.** The card that follows Flame with a hidden 9 just spent that 9 forever. Following cheap and leading your doubles is the trick-craft; the deduction-craft is noticing what *others* chose to burn.
- **Leads are questions.** Lead a symbol and watch who sloughs: a slough says "no card of mine carries it" — eight cards of that symbol are now mapped against their hand. The reveal then confirms it in public.
- **Call timing is the whole game.** Each trick's reveal removes 3–5 candidates. The expected value of waiting one more trick is usually less than the point you lose — *if* you're actually narrowing. Track the moment your candidate set hits one, and notice when an opponent's eyes say theirs has.

## Variants

- **Quiet Round** (first game): no calls; pure trick-taking with reveals, 1 point per trick. Learn the follow-by-either-face rhythm first.
- **Double Stakes:** a correct call scores +2 per unled trick, a wrong one −6. For tables that found their nerve.
- **The Coroner** (5P): the round's loser examines the unplayed card *alone* after scoring and shuffles it back in. Across a match, knowing past missing pairs is worth nothing — except to the player who notices someone relax.
