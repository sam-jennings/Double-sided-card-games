# Lesson 12: Marketing Strategy & Engagement

## TL;DR
- Discovery ranking: Influencers (YouTube/TikTok) → Friends → Social Media → BGG.
- Start marketing 3-4 months before launch; too early (6+) gets lost, too late kills momentum.
- Pre-launch: Announcement → Designer journals → Rulebook → Promo videos → Influencer previews → Ads → Conventions (weekly/biweekly cadence).
- Post-launch: Free content drops → Review copies to influencers (<50% return) → Paid playthroughs → Continued ads → Conventions → Tournaments.
- Brand identity: Always positive. One bad interaction ruins lifetime reputation. Avoid gendered/negative commentary online.
- Influencers: Match voice to game. Don't pressure for reviews. Diversify platforms (YouTube, podcasts, TikTok, blogs). Reviews should NOT be paid; preview videos ARE paid.
- Conventions: Gen Con, PAX Unplugged, UK Games Expo, Spiel Essen. Direct customer connection, hands-on play, word-of-mouth (method #2 discovery).
- Do something unique for marketing (interactive tool, create-your-own website) but don't over-commit to ongoing work.

## Core Concepts
- **Discovery Channels** — Influencers dominate (reach, engagement). Friends are #2 (word-of-mouth drives trials). Social media + BGG = direct reach but limited. Traditional marketing (ads, conventions) support tier.
- **Marketing Beats** — Distinct pieces of content drip-fed over time. Each release = new discovery opportunity. Week 1: announcement. Week 3: designer journal. Week 5: rulebook. Etc. Rhythm matters more than single big push.
- **Timing Window** — 3-4 months before launch = sweet spot. Retailers/distributors pay attention. Too early (6+) gets buried in noise. Too late (<3mo) insufficient buildup.
- **Brand Reputation** — Built over lifetime, destroyed by one interaction. Online comments, social media behavior, forum participation all contribute. Negative review/comment you leave could resurface when you're pitching. Maintain professional, positive voice always.
- **Influencer Ecosystem** — YouTube = largest reach but busiest. Podcasts = large, engaged audience, longer format. TikTok = huge board game presence, younger demographic. Bloggers, Instagram, Twitter = niche communities. No single channel dominates; diversify.
- **Content Types** — Announcement (press release). Designer journals (behind-the-scenes). Rulebook (marketing asset). Promo videos (simple sizzle reels). Tutorials (paid, like Watch It Played). Playthroughs (paid). Reviews (free to reviewer, not paid by you).

## Design Principles
- If launching → start marketing 3-4 months ahead
- If pitching influencers → match channel/voice to game tone
- If building brand → always be positive; one negative comment can resurface
- If short on budget → focus on organic (designer journals, convention demos, free content) over paid ads
- If crowdfunding → rulebook is critical (most backers won't back without it); must be polished
- Avoid pressure tactics → reviewers choose what to cover; unsolicited follow-ups damage relationships
- Prioritise authenticity → genuine enthusiasm for community > marketing-speak
- Do something unique → interactive website, create-your-own tool, etc. But don't over-commit

## Heuristics (Fast Decision Rules)
- **Marketing Timeline**: 3-4 months pre-launch. Not 6+ (gets lost), not <1mo (no buildup).
- **Content Cadence**: Weekly or biweekly posts pre-launch. Momentum builds through consistency, not volume.
- **Influencer Outreach**: Match voice to game first. Small channels = more responsive. Don't pressure; one rejection won't sink you.
- **Review vs. Preview**: Reviews = free to reviewer, unpaid by you. Preview videos/playthroughs = paid by you. Reviewers ask for payment? Red flag.
- **Bad Review Reality**: One negative review affects that influencer's audience only. Won't sink entire game. Keep making best game possible.
- **Convention ROI**: Direct customer interaction > paid ads. Word-of-mouth spreads through networks. Worth the booth cost for building buzz.

## Design Frameworks
- **Pre-Launch Campaign (3-4 month window)**:
  - **Month 1**: Announcement/press release (BGG news, gaming media)
  - **Week 2**: Designer journal #1 (game concept, theme, design philosophy)
  - **Week 4**: Designer journal #2 (mechanics reveal, gameplay loop)
  - **Week 6**: Release rulebook (free on website + BGG + Kickstarter)
  - **Week 8**: Promo videos (simple sizzle reels, component showcases, 1-2 minutes)
  - **Week 10**: Influencer previews launch (paid preview content from 2-3 channels)
  - **Week 12**: Ads begin (BGG ads, Facebook, gaming publications)
  - **Throughout**: Convention demos if timeline allows

- **Post-Launch Campaign (3-6 months)**:
  - **Month 1 post-launch**: Free content drops (puzzles, missions, strategy guides, designer commentary)
  - **Month 1-2**: Review copies sent to influencers (expect <50% to cover it)
  - **Month 1-3**: Paid playthrough/tutorial videos (if budget allows)
  - **Month 2-6**: Continued ad campaigns (maintain awareness, reach new audiences)
  - **Ongoing**: Convention attendance and demos
  - **Optional**: Tournaments (if game supports competitive play)

- **Target Audience Definition Template**:
  - Age range (young families, teens, adults, mixed)
  - Gaming preference (casual/party, strategy, narrative, competitive, co-op)
  - Existing interests (thematic IP fans, genre enthusiasts, hobby gamers vs. boardroom gamers)
  - Lifestyle (tabletop enthusiasts, video game crossover, book/comic fans)
  - Example: "Ages 14+, strategy game players, fans of fantasy IP and competitive gaming"

- **Competitive Analysis Framework**:
  - Identify 2-4 similar games (mechanics, theme, target audience)
  - For each: How is it positioned? Price point? Player count? Component quality? Theme? Complexity?
  - Your game's unique selling proposition (USP): What does yours have they don't? Simpler rules? More theme? Better components? Faster play? Deeper strategy?
  - Why customers choose your game over theirs (specific differentiators)

- **Marketing Channels (Ranked by Reach)**:
  - **Influencer channels** (YouTube, TikTok, podcasts, blogs)
  - **Social media** (Facebook groups, Twitter, Instagram, Reddit)
  - **BGG** (game page, forums, designer forums, top 100 lists)
  - **Direct email** (newsletter, once you have 2+ games)
  - **Paid advertising** (BGG ads, Facebook ads, gaming publications)
  - **Non-traditional spaces** (related hobby communities—fantasy fans, miniature painters, etc.)
  - **Conventions** (Gen Con, PAX, UK Games Expo, Spiel, regional cons)

## Common Pitfalls
- **Starting too early** → Marketing 6+ months before launch. Fails because: gets lost in noise before release relevance. Fix: Start 3-4 months out when retailers/distributors pay attention.
- **Releasing everything at once** → All marketing materials drop simultaneously. Fails because: no discovery window, no ongoing engagement, no new-discovery hooks. Fix: Drip-feed content over 3-4 months (weekly/biweekly cadence).
- **Pressuring influencers** → Sending follow-up emails demanding coverage after initial pitch. Fails because: damages relationship, influencers resent being pushed. Fix: Send pitch once, accept rejection gracefully, move on to next channel.
- **Paying for reviews** → Offering money for reviews. Fails because: unethical, damages credibility if discovered, influencers will reject or demand more. Fix: Reviews are unpaid; preview videos/playthroughs are paid.
- **Brand damage** → Negative comments, bad behavior on forums, dismissive tone toward community. Fails because: one bad interaction stays online forever, resurfaces when pitching, costs you reputation. Fix: Always be positive, professional, supportive. Assume all comments are public.
- **No unique element** → Generic "here's our game" marketing. Fails because: blends into thousands of other games. Fix: One memorable element (interactive website, create-your-own tool, unique social media voice). Don't over-commit—consistency > complexity.
- **Ignoring non-influencer channels** → Only chasing YouTube/TikTok. Fails because: some audiences only use podcasts, Reddit, or specific blogs. Fix: Diversify to 2-3 platforms matching your audience.
- **Missing convention opportunity** → Not demoing at conventions pre/post-launch. Fails because: word-of-mouth is #2 discovery method; direct play is most convincing. Fix: Hit 1-2 major cons + regional cons if geography allows.

## Connections

- **Lesson 10 (Rulebooks)** → Well-written rulebook is free marketing asset. Release publicly before launch. QR codes link to tutorial videos.
- **Lesson 11 (Manufacturing)** → Demo copies needed for convention buzz, influencer outreach. PoD or small runs allow early marketing before main print run.
- **Brand reputation** → Every online interaction counts. Comments on BGG, Twitter, forums all contribute to professional identity.

## Application
- **Marketing kickoff** (3-4 months pre-launch): Create editorial calendar. Announcement → designer journals (2x) → rulebook → promo videos → influencer previews → ads → conventions.
- **Influencer strategy**: Identify 5-10 channels matching your game's tone/audience. Research their process, send personalized pitch (not generic template). Accept rejection. Don't pressure.
- **Content creation** (minimal budget): Designer journals (free, behind-the-scenes), rules release (free marketing asset), simple promo videos (can be made with phone), convention presence (booth + direct demo).
- **Paid content** (if budget allows): Influencer preview videos (paid by you, pre-launch), tutorial videos (Watch It Played style, if game complexity justifies), playthrough videos (post-launch).
- **Unique element**: Interactive website (create-your-own scoundrel, mission selector), design-your-own mechanic tool, or community challenge. Cost low; commitment moderate; payoff high.
- **Convention strategy**: Gen Con/PAX = major reach but expensive. Regional cons = lower cost, engaged audience. Even small 2,000-person cons drive word-of-mouth.
- **Post-launch momentum**: Free content drops every 1-2 months (puzzles, guides, strategies). Keep game visible through continued engagement, not just sales push.
